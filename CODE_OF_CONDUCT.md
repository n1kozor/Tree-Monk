# Code of Conduct

TreeMonk is a small, friendly project. To keep it that way, everyone who takes
part — in issues, pull requests, discussions or any other project space — is
asked to follow a few simple rules.

## Be respectful

- Treat others with courtesy and good faith. Assume people mean well.
- Welcome newcomers and beginners. Everyone was new once.
- Critique ideas and code, not people. Keep feedback constructive and specific.
- Respect that this is a hobby project maintained in spare time — be patient.

## Not acceptable

- Harassment, insults, or discriminatory remarks of any kind (including those
  based on age, disability, ethnicity, gender, religion, nationality, sexual
  orientation, or similar).
- Personal attacks, sustained disruption, trolling, or deliberately derailing
  discussions.
- Publishing other people's private information (including the personal or
  genealogical data that this app is designed to keep private) without consent.
- Sexualized language or imagery, and unwelcome sexual attention.

## Scope

This applies to all project spaces (the repository, issues, pull requests and
any official channels) and to public situations where someone is representing
the project.

## Reporting

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, please report it privately
to the maintainer through **[treemonk.eu](https://treemonk.eu)** or by opening a
GitHub [security advisory](https://github.com/n1kozor/TreeMonk/security/advisories)
(which keeps the report private). Reports are handled confidentially.

## Enforcement

The maintainer may edit or remove contributions, and warn or block participants,
that violate this Code of Conduct, at their discretion. The goal is always to
keep the project a calm, useful and welcoming place.
